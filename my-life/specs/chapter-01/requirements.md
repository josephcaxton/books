# Requirements: Chapter 1 — The Blueprint of Purpose

## Purpose
Chapter 1 is the memoir heart of the book. It introduces the author's core argument about purpose through his own life story, establishes the spiritual and practical dual-awakening arc, and delivers the first set of concrete legacy instructions to the next generation.

---

## Content Requirements

### Requirement 1: The Lesson — Purpose Must Be Built, Not Found

**User Story:** As a reader, I want to understand the author's core belief about purpose, so that I am prepared for the personal stories that follow and understand what they are meant to teach.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. The lesson section SHALL establish that purpose is not stumbled upon — it must be discovered and then deliberately built.
2. The author SHALL acknowledge his past misconception (that purpose "just happens") before correcting it.
3. The section SHALL make clear that an externally imposed blueprint — even from well-meaning parents — will eventually crumble.
4. The section SHALL establish that purpose cannot thrive in spiritual or mental darkness — freedom is a prerequisite for building.
5. Tone SHALL be retrospective and honest, not preachy.

---

### Requirement 2: Story Part I — The Cloud of the Forced Path

**User Story:** As a reader, I want to understand the author's first major life disruption, so that I can see how an externally imposed path derailed his sense of direction and purpose.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. The story SHALL cover the transition from a thriving local school to a prestigious elite school.
2. The author SHALL acknowledge that his parents' intentions were good — this is not a story of blame.
3. The story SHALL describe the onset of "the darkness" — the loss of direction, confidence, and drive — without dramatisation or self-pity.
4. The consequences SHALL be stated plainly: disappointing exam results and years spent retaking papers.
5. The counterfactual — *"if they had left me at that first school"* — SHALL appear to anchor the lesson without lingering on regret.

---

### Requirement 3: Story Part II — The Warning, the Wilderness, and the Awakening

**User Story:** As a reader, I want to follow the author's journey through the military, the destructive wilderness period, and his dual awakening, so that I can understand how purpose and spiritual freedom were ultimately found.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. The stadium dream SHALL be described in specific, vivid detail (parade, military governors, missing tyres, half-built fence).
2. The author SHALL note that his initial interpretation of the dream was wrong, and that it only made sense in retrospect.
3. The military year SHALL be framed as incomplete but not wasted — yielding discipline, tenacity, confidence, courage, and independence.
4. The post-military wilderness (partying, drinking, bad company) SHALL be stated honestly without excusing or over-dramatising.
5. The DOS computer moment SHALL be described as an instant, unmistakable recognition of life purpose.
6. The revival and giving his life to Jesus SHALL be the spiritual turning point — described as a lifting of the darkness and a flooding of light.
7. The section SHALL end with the author departing abroad with a clear mind, discipline, and a spiritual foundation.

---

### Requirement 4: Advice to the Next Generation

**User Story:** As a reader (specifically a family member or descendant), I want clear, practical guidance drawn from the author's journey, so that I can apply these lessons without paying the same price.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. The advice section SHALL contain at least five distinct pieces of guidance, each drawn directly from an event in the chapter's stories.
2. Each piece of advice SHALL be introduced with a bold heading and explained in 2–4 sentences.
3. The spiritual advice (listening to God's whispers, seeking spiritual alignment) SHALL sit alongside practical advice without one overshadowing the other.
4. The parenting advice ("Guide, Never Decide") SHALL appear as the final point, closing the loop on the forced-path story.
5. Tone SHALL be warm and direct — a father speaking to children, not a preacher speaking to a congregation.

---

## Style Requirements

- First-person retrospective voice throughout.
- The dual-lens perspective must be present: what the author felt *then* vs. what he understands *now*.
- No passive victimhood — the author owns his wilderness years without blaming parents or circumstances.
- Christian framework maintained (God, Jesus, Holy Spirit, revival) — no vague spiritualism.
- No banned clichés.
