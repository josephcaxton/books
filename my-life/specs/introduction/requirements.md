# Requirements: Introduction — The Two Teachers

## Purpose
The introduction establishes the philosophical foundation of the entire book. It must immediately communicate the author's intent, define the two learning frameworks that underpin every chapter, and create an emotional contract with the reader — specifically the author's family and descendants.

## Content Requirements

### Requirement 1: The Two Teachers Framework

**User Story:** As a reader, I want to understand the two ways people learn lessons in life, so that I can immediately grasp the book's core philosophy before reading any chapter.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. The introduction SHALL present exactly two teachers: Instruction and Experience.
2. Instruction SHALL be described as the easier, humility-requiring path — learning from others' warnings before experiencing pain firsthand.
3. Experience SHALL be described as the harder, more expensive path — a teacher who gives the test before the lesson.
4. The contrast between the two SHALL be clear, memorable, and framed without condescension.

---

### Requirement 2: The Law of the Harvest

**User Story:** As a reader, I want to understand the principle of sowing and reaping, so that I grasp why living on autopilot leads to unintended outcomes.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. The Law of the Harvest SHALL be introduced as a fixed, universal principle — not a motivational concept.
2. The section SHALL include the core aphorism: you cannot plant thorns and expect to harvest grapes.
3. The section SHALL link autopilot living directly to the failure to reach a self-chosen destination.
4. The tone SHALL be declarative and grounded, not preachy or abstract.

---

### Requirement 3: The Legacy Dedication

**User Story:** As a reader (specifically a family member or descendant), I want to understand who this book is written for and why, so that I feel the personal weight and urgency of the author's message.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. The introduction SHALL explicitly state the book is written for the author's family and future generations.
2. The author SHALL acknowledge his own experience-paid scars honestly, without self-pity or victimhood.
3. The author's goal — transferring wisdom from Experience to Instruction — SHALL be stated directly.
4. The reader SHALL be told the book is intentionally short and concise, and why.
5. The tone SHALL feel like a father or grandfather speaking, warm and urgent, not academic.

---

## Style Requirements

- Written in first-person retrospective voice.
- No clichés (e.g., "everything happens for a reason").
- Aphoristic, punchy sentences balanced with moments of warmth.
- Must not exceed the length of the existing draft — the introduction should be short enough to read in under five minutes.
