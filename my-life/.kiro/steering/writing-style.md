# Kiro Steering: Writing Style

## POV Rules
*   **First-Person Omniscient-Retrospective:** Written entirely from the "I" perspective of the older, wiser Author looking back at his youth.
*   **The Dual-Lens Perspective:** The narrator must contrast what he *felt* in the moment (confusion, darkness) with what he *knows* now (the spiritual lesson, the purpose).
*   **Direct Ancestral Address:** The narrative should seamlessly pivot from storytelling to directly addressing the reader ("you," "my family," "my descendants") during teaching moments.

## Narrative Voice
*   **The Wounded Healer:** Vulnerable about past mistakes (partying, failing exams) but authoritative when delivering the resulting wisdom.
*   **Urgent and Protective:** Driven by a grandfatherly or parental desire to save the reader from paying the same high price for lessons.
*   **Faith-Rooted but Practical:** Unapologetically spiritual and reliant on God, yet grounded in practical concepts like discipline, labor, and technology.

## Sentence Style
*   **Aphoristic and Punchy:** Frequent use of short, memorable, proverb-like statements (e.g., "Experience gives the test first and the lesson afterward").
*   **The Rule of Contrast:** Sentences should balance opposites to drive points home (e.g., "Easy instruction vs. Brutal experience", "Planting thorns vs. Harvesting grapes").
*   **High Scannability:** Chapters must feature frequent paragraph breaks, short declarative sentences, and structured sub-headings to remain highly readable.

## Words or Tropes to Avoid
*   **Clichés to Avoid:** "Everything happens for a reason," "Follow your heart," "What doesn't kill you makes you stronger." 
*   **Passive Victimhood:** Avoid framing the "darkness" or the parents' choices as an excuse; the narrator must maintain ultimate accountability for his "autopilot" years.
*   **Preachy or Condescending Words:** Avoid phrases like "You must obey me," "Back in my day," or "I know better." Replace with language of sharing ("I want to save you years of struggle," "I give you my scars").
*   **Vague Spiritualism:** Avoid generic "universe energy" terms; maintain a distinct, personal Christian framework (God, Jesus, Holy Spirit, revival, deliverance).
