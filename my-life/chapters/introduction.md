# Introduction: The Two Teachers

Life is a continuous process of schooling, but the curriculum is delivered by only two teachers: Instruction and Experience.

## 1. The Teacher of Instruction

This is the "easy" way. It is the warning on the stove, the map in the glovebox, and the advice of a parent. Learning by instruction requires humility. The ability to believe someone else's "no" before you feel the burn yourself. It is a shortcut to wisdom, allowing you to stand on the shoulders of those who came before you.

## 2. The Teacher of Experience

This is the "hard" way. Experience is a beautiful but brutal teacher; she gives the test first and the lesson afterward. While these lessons are often the ones that stick with us the longest because they are paid for in tears, time, or money, they are also the most expensive.

## The Law of the Harvest

A lot of us walk through life blindly. We set ourselves on autopilot, reacting to things as they happen rather than acting with purpose. But no matter how you choose to manage your life, your perspective won't change the fundamental laws of how this world is designed.

The universe operates on a fixed principle: Whatsoever you sow, you will reap. You cannot plant thorns and expect to harvest grapes. You cannot live on autopilot and expect to arrive at a destination of your own choosing.

## A Legacy for My Family

I am writing this book specifically for my family. For those who will survive me and for the generations still to come. I have spent much of my life being a student of experience. I have the scars, the missed opportunities, and the "I wish I had known" to prove it.

There is a specific kind of wisdom that only comes from "scars," and giving that to you can save you years of unnecessary struggle. My goal for this book is to move as many lessons as possible from the category of "Experience" into the category of "Instruction".

I want you to have my wisdom without having to pay my price. I intentionally kept this book short and concise. My goal was to make it easy to read so that you can keep it close by and constantly return to these pages to refresh your mind.
